IABE 2025 - IJBR 2025

Deception using social media for cryptocurrency fraud

Keywords: Decentralized finance, cryptocurrency exchange failures, market manipulation

Exploring how bias information influence invester actions and influence on cryptocurrency exchange valuation

Thousands of investors are being targeted daily by cryptocurrency fraudsters, losing millions to schemes that take advantage of workers’ hard-earned money and misinformation. One of the main drivers of these scams is social media, specifically YouTube, where creators, whether knowingly or unknowingly, promote scams to a huge number of viewers. Millions act upon their recommendations, and these creators drive investment decisions, often promoting questionable enterprises to a large audience without due care. This study investigates the developing problem of crypto fraud driven through influencers by analyzing psychological influences in audiences and ethical concerns deriving from endorsements that pose regulatory challenges.

Presented orally at the IABE Conference 2025 and accepted for publication in the International Journal of Business Research.

ICAEB 2025

Primal-Dual Optimization for Supply-Chains

Keywords: Supply chain management, stochastic modeling, supply chain optimization

A new algorithmic approach for reducing uncertainty and inefficiency in modern supply networks.

Distributed supply‑chain optimization demands algorithms that can cope with unreliable communication, unbounded messaging delays, and geographically dispersed agents, yet still guarantee convergence with practical rates. In this work, we introduce DAPD‑SCO (Distributed Asynchronous Primal–Dual Optimization Supply‑Chain Optimization), a fully asynchronous primal–dual scheme for network flow allocation over directed acyclic supply‑chain graphs. Each edge‑agent independently updates its local flow via projected gradient descent, while each retailer‑agent adjusts its dual multiplier via projected gradient ascent, using only potentially stale information of arbitrary sublinear delay. Under standard convexity and Slater’s conditions—and without any global synchronization or bounded‑delay assumptions—we prove almost sure convergence to a saddle point and establish an ergodic duality‑gap rate of \(O(K^{-1/2})\), matching centralized lower bounds. Our analysis leverages a Lyapunov‑based decomposition that cleanly isolates delay‑induced errors and handles time‑varying communication topologies, bounded noise, and drifting cost or capacity parameters. Extensive simulations on realistic three‑tier networks demonstrate that DAPD‑SCO outperforms synchronous primal–dual, Asynchronous Distributed Decision-Making (ADDM), and gradient‑push approaches, achieving faster convergence, lower communication overhead, and robust performance under packet loss and high staleness.

Accepted to ICAEB 2025.

ICML 2025

Data Cartography for Impartial LLM Evaluation

Keywords: LLM safety, pruning, visualization

Creating fair evaluation benchmarks using data maps and memorization-aware pruning for large models.

Modern generative models risk overfitting and unintentionally memorizing rare training examples, which can be extracted by adversaries or inflate benchmark performance. We propose Generative Data Cartography (GenDataCarto), a data-centric framework that assigns each pretraining sample a difficulty score (early-epoch loss) and a memorization score (frequency of “forget events”), then partitions examples into four quadrants to guide targeted pruning and up-/down-weighting. We prove that our memorization score lower-bounds classical influence under smoothness assumptions and that down-weighting high-memorization hotspots provably decreases the generalization gap via uniform stability bounds. Empirically, GenDataCarto reduces synthetic canary extraction success by over 40% at just 10% data pruning, while increasing validation perplexity by less than 0.5%. These results demonstrate that principled data interventions can dramatically mitigate leakage with minimal cost to generative performance.

Accepted to the 42nd International Conference on Machine Learning; youngest invitee in ICML history.

Economics behind high density energy storage for sodium-ion battery

Keywords: Density storage optimization, material science, microeconomics

Combining economic and material analysis to evaluate the viability of sodium-ion energy storage systems for implementation into the data center

This project details the economics behind developing a sodium-ion battery from extraction, purification, etc to implementation into the data center. Currently lithium-ion batteries are being used as the primary source of energy, this research compares the economics behind the production of both batteries and weights the pros and cons of both to present the sodium-ion battery as a subsitute. Factors like pollution, harm, and other factors are also considered.
